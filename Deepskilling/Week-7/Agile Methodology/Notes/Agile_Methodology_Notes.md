# Agile Methodology Fundamentals

## 1. Introduction to Agile

Agile is a flexible software development approach that emphasizes iterative development, close collaboration, customer involvement, and continuous improvement. It enables teams to deliver valuable software in smaller, manageable increments.

### Objectives of Agile

- Deliver software regularly
- Respond quickly to changing requirements
- Improve customer satisfaction
- Encourage collaboration among team members
- Enhance product quality

---

## 2. Agile Manifesto

### Four Core Values

- Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
- Working software over extensive documentation
- Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
- Responding to change over following a fixed plan

---

### Key Agile Principles

- Deliver valuable software early and continuously.
- Welcome changes, even during development.
- Release working software frequently.
- Promote close collaboration between business and development teams.
- Build projects with motivated individuals.
- Prefer direct communication whenever possible.
- Measure progress through working software.
- Maintain a sustainable pace of development.
- Focus on technical excellence and good design.
- Keep solutions as simple as possible.
- Encourage self-organizing teams.
- Continuously evaluate and improve processes.

---

## 3. Agile vs Waterfall

| Agile | Waterfall |
|--------|-----------|
| Incremental and iterative | Sequential approach |
| Easily adapts to changes | Difficult to change once started |
| Continuous customer feedback | Feedback mainly at project completion |
| Frequent software releases | One final software release |
| High customer involvement | Limited customer participation |

---

## 4. Scrum Framework

Scrum is one of the most commonly adopted Agile frameworks for managing software projects.

### Scrum Roles

#### Product Owner

- Defines product goals
- Manages the Product Backlog
- Prioritizes business requirements

#### Scrum Master

- Guides the Scrum process
- Removes obstacles faced by the team
- Ensures Agile practices are followed

#### Development Team

- Designs the solution
- Develops features
- Tests the application
- Delivers working increments

---

## 5. Scrum Events

### Sprint Planning

Defines the sprint objective and selects backlog items.

### Daily Scrum

A short daily meeting to discuss:

- Completed work
- Planned work
- Current challenges

### Sprint Review

Demonstrates completed work to stakeholders for feedback.

### Sprint Retrospective

Reviews the sprint and identifies areas for improvement.

---

## 6. Scrum Artifacts

### Product Backlog

A prioritized list of all project requirements.

### Sprint Backlog

Tasks selected for the current sprint.

### Product Increment

A completed, usable portion of the product delivered after a sprint.

### Definition of Done

A checklist that determines whether work is complete.

---

## 7. Agile Estimation

### Story Points

Story points estimate the complexity or effort required for a user story.

Example Scale:

- 1 – Very Easy
- 2 – Easy
- 3 – Moderate
- 5 – Complex
- 8 – Very Complex
- 13 – Highly Complex

### Planning Poker

Planning Poker is a collaborative estimation technique where team members assign story points using numbered cards.

Advantages:

- Improves estimation accuracy
- Encourages team discussion
- Minimizes estimation bias

---

## 8. Sprint Planning

Sprint Planning focuses on:

- Defining the sprint goal
- Selecting work items
- Estimating effort
- Setting sprint duration

---

## 9. Velocity

Velocity measures the amount of work completed during a sprint using story points.

Example:

Sprint 1 → 25 Story Points

Sprint 2 → 30 Story Points

Average Velocity = 27.5 Story Points

---

## 10. Burndown Chart

A Burndown Chart tracks the remaining work throughout a sprint and helps determine whether the project is progressing according to schedule.

---

## 11. User Stories

A User Story captures a feature from the end user's perspective.

Format:

**As a** user

**I want** a feature

**So that** I achieve a benefit

Example:

As a customer,

I want to reset my password,

So that I can access my account again.

---

## 12. INVEST Principle

Effective user stories should follow the INVEST guideline:

- Independent
- Negotiable
- Valuable
- Estimable
- Small
- Testable

---

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

Acceptance Criteria define the conditions that must be satisfied before a user story is considered complete.

Example:

**Given** the user is on the login page

**When** valid login credentials are entered

**Then** the dashboard should be displayed.

---

## 14. Agile Collaboration

Agile teams achieve success through:

- Regular communication
- Daily Scrum meetings
- Continuous customer feedback
- Shared ownership
- Continuous improvement

---

## Conclusion

Agile is an adaptive software development methodology that focuses on collaboration, iterative delivery, and customer value. Scrum practices, estimation techniques, user stories, and continuous feedback help teams build high-quality software efficiently.
